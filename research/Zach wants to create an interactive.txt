I'm considering alternatives, however, because of Dr. Moritz Lehmann's "FluidX3D" that is written in C++ and uses OpenCL instead of CUDA.

For the supported operating systems, I think my plan is to mainly support Windows first. However, I could do native Linux testing via either WSLv2 or an installation of something such as POP!_OS on an external SanDisk extreme SSD. Therefore I should probably prioritize cross-platform supported libraries.
